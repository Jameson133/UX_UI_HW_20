Department of Labor Redesign

An analysis, critique, and redesign of the UI for the Department of Labor website.

My Role:

UX Designer and Researcher on a team of 4

Background:

In this project, we tasked ourselves to evaluate and improve upon the UI of a government website.

Deliverables:

User interviews, data synthesis and analysis, persona creation, story board, user journey mapping, user flows, wireframing, prototyping, and usability testing and iteration.

Goal:

To improve upon the user interface of the Department of Labor website and RWD.

Measure of Success

Success will be recognized through test results of the same tasks before and after the iterations to the current design.

What I Did

The Department of Labor is the most direct source of information regarding all rights and regulations for wage earners. Therefore, the importance of updating information and keeping the site usability at a high level can’t be understated. When a new business owner or a laborer go to the website, why does it take so much effort to find the information they are looking for?

My research team sought to improve the accessibility of information by first testing the intuitive design of the existing website.

The Process

The first step we took was to redline the current website in order to aid us in identifying information overlap and the components that went into the existing design.

We also performed a Heuristic Evaluation to give us a direction for where the greatest impact can be made.

We performed a Color Accessibility Test on the existing site in order to gather if the color pallet used was fitting for the wide-reaching audience the site must accommodate to. (it passed)

We then made a Proto Persona. This enabled us to empathize with and identify potential users, and gain a better idea of potential user paths.

User Research & Findings

Usability Tasks:

Task 1 - Locate and Reearch Hiring Practices
Task 2 - Using this website, find out the minimum wage in your state
Task 3 - Find the Mission Statement
Task 4 - Find the Document for the Deparmtnet of Labor Operating Budget

Our group mapped out 4 user paths to go with each of our usability test tasks on the original website.

We conducted Guerilla User Testing and sorted the results into a prioritization matrix to align the needs of the users with that of the Dept. of Labor. Users struggled to complete the 4 tasks assigned.

The primary pain points were identified through the user testing, and again sorted in a priority matrix.

Ideation Design

We compiled inspiration for the redesign into a moodboard to better follow the mission of the Dept. of Labor moving forward with the redesign.

At this point in the project my team shifted to individual redesign iterations. I chose to first address the primary navigation errors through Card Sorting the navigation links.

The new card sorting categories added more navigation options to the primary menu, but it also cleaned up a lot of the overlapping information. By doing this I was also able to improve the user success on the previously tested tasks.

I then crafted a new Site Map to implement the results of the Card Sorting. This served as the structure for my prototype.

I began the Wireframing process by recreating the Homepage and displaying the new navigation according to the Site Map. I then took the opportunity to rework the sub navigation menus on the informational pages, and streamline the process of accessing the information in an orderly manner.

My next step was to take the Wireframe out of Low Fidelity through creating a Design Style Guide. I made my decisions of color and iconography based on current standards of practice, the inspirational moodboard my team made, and the mission of the Dept. of Labor.

Lastly, I applied the style guide to the wireframe.

Usability Testing & Findings

User Test


I followed my redesign with further usability testing to confirm that my adjustments and redesign was more effective than the original.

I used a Frequency of Response table to record and categorize the results of my User Testing. I discovered that the original tasks assigned from the original website were completed much easier in the redesign, but there were some adjustments still to be made.

I applied the frequency of response user test results to a Priority Matrix and was able to narrow down the path of my most necessary iterations.

Closing & Complete Case Study

• Sorting and Prioritizing content is critical for a site to offer an informative and enjoyable experience.

• Responsive Web Designs are essential features for designs in today’s day and age. Organizations lose far too many opportunities for new users from simply not adapting their websites to mobile and visa versa.

• Clarifying a site’s design style and brand gives the opportunity for consistency across platforms and user familiarity with the designs. This rewards the users with an earned sense of understanding and foresight when using these products in the future.
